How to create a meme — step-by-step

Simple, numbered process with short descriptions and small tips.

  1. Pick an idea or joke

    Decide what reaction, punchline, or commentary you want—relatable emotions and short, specific concepts work best.

    Tip: keep it sharable — one clear idea per meme.

  2. Choose a format or template

    Select an image or short video (classic template, reaction shot, screenshot, or your own photo). Recognizable templates help virality.

    Tip: search “meme templates” if you need inspiration.

  3. Write concise text

    Add a short, punchy caption. Use line breaks for timing. Keep wording simple and easy to read at a glance.

    Micro-rule: under 8–12 words per line usually reads fast.

  4. Design & layout

    Place text over or under the image using readable font (white text + thin dark outline or drop shadow is common). Maintain contrast and avoid clutter.

    Common fonts: Impact, Arial Bold, or system sans with strong stroke.

  5. Edit & resize

    Crop and resize for your platform (Instagram, Twitter, Discord). Save a high-quality PNG/JPEG; for short loops use MP4/WebM.

    Tip: 1200×1200 (square) or 1080×1350 (portrait) works well on many platforms.

  6. Optional: add watermark or credit

    If you want recognition, add a small handle or logo. Keep it subtle so it doesn't distract from the joke.

  7. Export & test

    Export at optimal compression. Preview on phone and desktop to check readability and cropping.

  8. Post & share

    Post on the platforms where your audience is. Use a short caption, relevant tags, and consider timing for maximum reach.

  9. Iterate from feedback

    Watch responses. If a meme lands, analyze why; if it doesn’t, tweak wording or image for future attempts.
